# RED GARDEN — Backstory: The Gang

*Written: 2026-07-23 | Status: lore, cross-referenced from docs/HEROES_VS0.md, not implemented in code*

## What this is

The in-fiction reason the nine S-tier heroes (`NORTHSTAR.md` §7) are a roster at all, rather than
nine unrelated characters somebody happened to draft into a card game. Tyler — the same Tyler as
`TYLER/` proper, drifting between his own show and whatever's nearby, per the standing rule that he
never completes a self-defining sentence about what he actually is — put a motorcycle gang together.
Nobody agreed to call it that on purpose. It's just what nine people who ride together long enough
end up being called by everyone who isn't them.

## How it actually started

Tyler doesn't recruit. He shows up somewhere, doesn't explain why, and by the time anyone thinks to
ask, whoever he showed up near has decided to come along. Every member of the gang has a version of
this same story, and none of the versions agree on what year it happened, which nobody in the gang
finds strange, because none of them were ever confident about the year either.

**The Donkey** folded up the day it met him and didn't unfold again for six weeks — the longest it's
ever stayed folded before or since. When it finally did, Tyler was still there, waiting, having
apparently not noticed six weeks had passed. That's the whole recruitment story. Neither of them has
ever elaborated on it.

**The Duck** told Tyler, unprompted, about its government job. Tyler said he believed it. The Duck
has brought this up at every opportunity since, to everyone, as proof that somebody finally took it
seriously — Tyler maintains he wasn't being polite, he genuinely doesn't have a basis to disbelieve a
duck about anything, which the Duck has decided to also take as a compliment.

**The Unicorn** ran a diagnostic on him the first time they met. The diagnostic came back
inconclusive, which the Unicorn found more suspicious than a clean failure would have been — you can
disprove a lie, you can't disprove a result that just refuses to resolve. It joined to keep running
the diagnostic. It is still running.

**The Ghost** was mid-piano when Tyler walked in, and didn't stop playing, and Tyler didn't ask it to.
That's the entire basis of their friendship: neither one has ever needed the other to explain what
they actually are, on the theory that whatever it is, it's clearly working.

**The Frog** has never told Tyler its secret either, same as everyone else, and reports that Tyler is
the only member of the gang who's never once asked — which the Frog finds more unsettling than the
asking would be, since it raises the possibility that Tyler already knows and simply doesn't need
telling.

**The Tree** hasn't moved since. It joined the gang the way trees join anything, which is to say the
gang formed around where it already was, and everyone agreed calling that "joining" was close enough.
It still speaks only French about the one subject that matters, untranslated, and the gang has stopped
asking someone to translate.

**The Pizza** was already on fire. It has been on fire this entire time, including before the gang,
during the gang, and — everyone assumes — after. It rides in the sidecar. Nobody has investigated why
the sidecar hasn't burned. Investigating is against the one house rule the gang has ever agreed on
out loud: don't look directly at the Pizza's situation, it's handling it.

**The Retrieval Cart** doesn't ride with the gang so much as arrive wherever the gang already is,
carrying something nobody asked for, on a schedule nobody set. The gang has taken to treating this as
membership anyway, on the grounds that showing up with unrequested utility, over and over, without
fail, is a more reliable definition of loyalty than anything a formal invitation could establish.

**Doc Wheel**, last to join and first to be trusted, patched up whichever member of the gang was worst
off the week it arrived and never brought it up again. It has no interest in being the center of the
story. It is, by wide agreement within the gang, the only member anyone would actually want beside
them if something went wrong — precisely because it's never once acted like that mattered to it.

## Why a motorcycle gang, specifically

None of them own a motorcycle in the conventional sense. The Donkey folds. The Ghost passes through
things. The Tree hasn't moved in years. What they share isn't the vehicle, it's the shape of the
thing: a loose column of very different riders, no formal hierarchy, held together by nothing except
the fact that they keep ending up going the same direction as Tyler, for reasons none of them can
fully account for and none of them have felt the need to press him on. Tyler carries things until
somebody else is standing where he isn't (see `TYLER/episodes/x00_the_custody_of_a_duck.md`); the
gang is nine separate answers to the question of who that somebody turned out to be, gathered
without ever being formally asked.

## Why this explains the roster

Every card in `docs/HEROES_VS0.md` is a member of this gang, not nine strangers a deck-builder
happened to draft. The gang is the deck. Drawing a card and deploying it onto the board is, in-
fiction, calling in whichever rider is nearest — which is also why several of them (the Tree, the
Pizza, the Retrieval Cart, Doc Wheel) leave a mark on the living grid itself rather than just
fighting on it: the gang was never really visiting the board. It was always already there.
